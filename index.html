House

A house is a place where people live, offering shelter and comfort. It typically includes rooms like bedrooms, a living room, kitchen, and bathroom. Houses can vary in size, style, and design, but they all provide a space for relaxation, safety, and personal activities.

Playground

Playground

A playground is an outdoor area designed for children to play and have fun. It usually features equipment like swings, slides, climbing structures, and sandboxes. Playgrounds encourage physical activity, social interaction, and imagination, making them important for children's development.

Church

Church

A church is a building used for Christian worship and religious activities. It serves as a place for prayer, reflection, and community gatherings. Churches often feature religious symbols, altars, and seating for congregants, and they play an important role in spiritual and social life.

Town Hall

Town Hall

A town hall is a public building where local government meetings and community events are held. It serves as a center for civic activities, offering a space for citizens to meet with officials, discuss issues, and engage in local decision-making. Town halls are important for fostering communication within a community.

School

School

A school is an institution where students receive education and learn various subjects, such as math, science, language, and arts. Schools provide a structured environment for academic growth, social development, and skill-building, preparing students for future careers and life challenges.

Wet Market

Wet Market

A wet market is a type of marketplace that sells fresh food, including meat, seafood, vegetables, and fruits. The term "wet" refers to the presence of water used for cleaning products and maintaining freshness. Wet markets are common in many parts of Asia and offer locally sourced, often affordable food items.
